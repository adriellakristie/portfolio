THE PROBLEM

Eating out is at the heart of New York culture, and the carbon impact of every food decision adds up — invisibly, meal after meal.

THE SOLUTION

Reshaping the culture of eating by creating a social platform to promote sustainable eating habits — making the greenest choice the obvious one.

How It Works

Every restaurant is assigned an EcoBite Green Score, built from public city data and weighted across three factors:

Energy Efficiency
50%
Water Efficiency
25%
Health
25%

Data sources: NYC LL84 Building Energy, DOHMH Inspection

Carbon Impact = Average Score − Ordered Score, converted to kg CO₂ saved per choice.

Market Analysis

Apps like Beli have proven appetite for the "social network for foodies" model — users sharing and discovering restaurants together — growing from 200,000 monthly active users in early 2024 to over 1.1 million by late 2025. EcoBite takes that same social mechanic and points it toward a sustainability movement: a way to make eco-conscious eating something people share, not just something they're told to do.
